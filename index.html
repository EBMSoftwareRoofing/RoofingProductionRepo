Methodology

How we read the industry

The US roofing industry runs on four interconnected components: materials producers and brands, distributors moving product from manufacturer to job site, contractors installing and maintaining roofing systems, and specialty tech reshaping how the work is sold, measured, and delivered. Sitting above all four is the macroeconomic environment — interest rates, housing starts, insurance dynamics, and the policy backdrop that shapes every transaction in the chain. Every issue of The Square Foot reads the month through this lens.

Macroeconomic Outlook

01

Roofing Materials

Companies that produce and distribute roofing products designed to cover, protect, and insulate residential and commercial structures — shingles, underlayment, decking, fasteners, and gutter/drainage systems. We track price action, raw input costs, and manufacturer-led pricing announcements.

02

Roofing Distributors

The specialized wholesale supply chain connecting manufacturers to contractors. Distributors operate networks of warehouses, fleet vehicles, and credit facilities — aggregating purchasing power to negotiate manufacturer rebates while serving as primary supply depot for regional installers.

03

Roofing Contractors

Businesses specializing in the installation, repair, maintenance, and waterproofing of roofing systems. Operate localized labor crews, specialized equipment, and direct customer relationships — the primary service provider layer for residential and commercial roofing work.

04

Roofing Specialty Tech

The blending of traditional roofing with digital, material, and robotic technologies — drones, AI-powered measurement, next-gen solar shingles, smart materials and sensors, and the operating-system platforms (CRM, estimating, field) reshaping how the industry runs.
